Global Music Culture

How did popular music around the world evolve over the last century? Is technology and birth of the internet changing the way music spreads? GlobalPOP project aims to answer these questions. Compiling a large-scale and comprehensive dataset of music aross 58 countries, GlobalPOP is the first to quantify the history of popular music culture in a data-driven manner.

*Website launch and open data release coming soon in 2025*

Longitudinal history of popular music across 55 countries, dating back to the 1950s. The data collection involved the manual transcription of over 5,000 library archival copies that had never been digitised, along with extensive web-scraped data including acoustic properties, metadata, and lyrics.

Cultural Spread

Ideas and information spread across networks of people. There can be many underlying mechanisms that drive the spread from social contagion between individuals to cultural diffusions across countries. By analysing the vast amount of data on our behaviour from the real world, we can begin to understand how these mechanisms work.

New online experimental frameworkes such as PsyNet, enables complex experimental designs that could not be done previously. For example, putting people in to a chain where one's response gets passed to another. You can also design a virtual micro-society to study how the possible interactions between individuals shape the overall behaviour of the society. This technological innovations, hand in hand with the real world data, allows to really understand the causal mechanisms behind how things spread.
